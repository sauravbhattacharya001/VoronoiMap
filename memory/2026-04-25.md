

## Research Paper Idea — Consensus-Driven Metacognition (9:13 PM)
- Started from Shubho's observation: "AI is what knowledge it thinks it knows"
- Key insight: single LLMs lack epistemic self-awareness — can't distinguish know vs infer vs fabricate
- Shubho's thesis: **two LLMs collaborating with consensus as the protocol = emergent metacognition**
  - Disagreement = system's version of doubt
  - Agreement = confidence signal
  - The consensus protocol IS the "observer" / "the I"
  - Not inside either model, but emergent in the space between them
- Analogous to human brain: parliament of modules competing, consensus emerging from conflict
- Key question: does diversity of models matter? (same model copies might agree on same mistakes)
- Working title: "Consensus-Driven Metacognition in Multi-Agent Systems"
- Research questions drafted:
  1. Can multi-agent disagreement approximate epistemic uncertainty?
  2. What consensus protocol best drives safe agency?
  3. Does model diversity improve the metacognitive signal?
  4. Can consensus serve as governance for autonomous agents?
- Fits Shubho's AI governance/safety research lane + CSA work
- **Next step:** Shubho wants to write this paper. Draft saved, continuing on desktop.
