## About the Conference

ICGIS 2026 brings together researchers, practitioners, and thought leaders to explore transformative innovations across artificial intelligence, distributed computing, trust systems, and cybersecurity.

Building on the success of ICGIS 2025—which featured 49 peer-reviewed papers published by Springer—this year's conference expands into four focused tracks addressing the most pressing challenges in intelligent systems and their integration into society.

**Proceedings published by Springer** in the Advances in Computer Science Applications and Research (ACSAR) series.

---

## Conference Tracks

### Track 1: Intelligent Agents & Cognitive Systems
*June 6, 2026 | Track Chair: Sairohith*

Exploring models and architectures for artificial cognition, reasoning, and autonomous decision-making. Topics include:
- Large language models and reasoning systems
- Multi-agent coordination and collaboration
- Autonomous planning and goal-directed behavior
- Human-AI interaction and cognitive architectures

### Track 2: Compute, Data & Distributed Infrastructure
*June 7, 2026 | Track Chair: Gaurav*

Scalable platforms and systems that power large-scale intelligent workloads. Topics include:
- Cloud and edge computing for AI
- Data pipelines and MLOps
- Distributed training and inference
- High-performance computing for machine learning

### Track 3: Identity, Trust & Socio-Technical Governance
*June 6, 2026 | Track Chair: Saurav Bhattacharya*

Foundations for identity, authority, accountability, and coordination among humans and machines. Topics include:
- Digital identity and authentication for AI agents
- Trust frameworks and reputation systems
- AI governance and policy
- Accountability in autonomous systems
- Human-machine coordination

### Track 4: Security, Safety & Adversarial Systems
*June 7, 2026 | Track Chair: Akshay*

Understanding and defending against failures, attacks, and misuse in intelligent systems. Topics include:
- Adversarial machine learning
- AI safety and alignment
- Cybersecurity for AI systems
- Privacy-preserving machine learning
- Robustness and reliability

## Call for Papers

We invite original research contributions across all four tracks. Submissions should present novel ideas, methods, or applications in the areas of intelligent systems, infrastructure, governance, and security.

**Submission Guidelines:**
- Papers must be original and not under review elsewhere
- Maximum 15 pages in Springer LNCS format
- All submissions will undergo double-blind peer review
- Accepted papers will be published in Springer proceedings
